Pre-Listing Prep

Stage 02  ·  Right after we agree to work together

Action Items

Kelsie will ask you for a handful of specific things so the listing can be put together cleanly. These are the ones that trip up sellers who leave them for later.

Please gather

  • Mortgage payoff amount from your lender, so the net sheet is real
  • HOA contact info, monthly dues, and any HOA docs, if applicable
  • A spare house key for the lockbox before we go live
  • Any recent service records for the roof, HVAC, water heater, or major systems

Once these are in, Kelsie assembles the net sheet, the MLS input sheet, and the pre-listing checklist.

Stage 03  ·  Nailing down the listing details

MLS Input Sheet

This is the sheet the MLS pulls from. Kelsie will send it and walk you through the fields that matter. It covers square footage, HOA info, included appliances, features, and the notes that shape how the listing reads to buyers.

Accuracy here matters. Buyers compare listings by these numbers, and any miss shows up in an inspection or appraisal later.
Stage 04  ·  The most important two hours in the listing

Photo Day

Photos decide showings. Kelsie's photo day guide covers what to do the morning of, and it is not optional prep.

The morning of, at a minimum

  • Every shade and blind open
  • Every light on, warm white bulbs matched throughout
  • Counters at three items or fewer, kitchen and baths
  • Toilet lids closed, everywhere
  • Cars off the driveway and off the front curb
  • Trash cans out of sight, pet gear stashed
  • Family out of the house during the shoot when possible

Stage 05  ·  Listing on the MLS

Going Live

Once the MLS input sheet is confirmed and photos are edited, the listing goes live. From there, Kelsie coordinates showings, tracks feedback, and keeps you in the loop.

Two things Kelsie needs from you in the first week: quick turnaround on any showing requests, and honest read on how the home is showing when you are home. Anything that feels off, tell Kelsie.

Stage 06  ·  Reading the offer, not just the number

Offers Coming In

When offers arrive, Kelsie will present them and walk through the terms with you. Price is one variable. Financing type, contingencies, closing timeline, and included items all matter to your final result.

The strongest offer is not always the highest. Kelsie will show you the trade-offs and give you a straight recommendation.
Stage 07  ·  Roadmap from acceptance to close

Under Contract

Once you accept an offer, several things happen in parallel over the next few weeks.

  1. Due diligence window: the buyer conducts inspections. You may receive a repair request. Kelsie will help you decide what to accept, negotiate, or decline.
  2. Appraisal: the buyer's lender orders one. Leave the home show-ready, you do not need to be there.
  3. Loan underwriting: runs in the background. No action needed from you.
  4. Final walkthrough: the buyer walks the home 24 to 48 hours before closing.
  5. Closing: you sign at the title company. Funds transfer. Done.

Kelsie will coordinate signatures and paperwork with you along the way, and she is always one text away for judgment calls.

Stage 08  ·  Review before signing

Closing Disclosures

Your seller closing disclosures will be issued by title before signing day. Read them carefully. If anything looks off, ask Kelsie before you sign.

Kelsie will confirm your signing time with you directly.

Stage 09  ·  The finish line

Closing Day

You will sign at the title company. Bring a government-issued photo ID. Plan for about an hour of signing.

Two housekeeping items

  • Keep utilities in your name until the day of possession, not before
  • Handle your address change after closing, USPS forward, driver's license update, banks, subscriptions
Recording is not always same-day. If signing happens late Friday, keys may pass Monday. Kelsie will keep you updated the moment the deed records.
Stage 10  ·  The post-closing follow-up

After Close

Once you are closed, Kelsie will send a short review request. If working together was helpful, a Google or Zillow review is the biggest thank you there is.

And one year in, expect a check-in from Kelsie with a light market update for your old neighborhood. No pitch, just good information to have.
