Stage 08

Deposit Your Earnest Money

To keep your contract on track, earnest money must be submitted within 4 calendar days of acceptance. You have three options:

  1. Cashier's Check, Obtain from your bank made out to the title company and deliver directly.
  2. Wire Transfer, Send funds electronically. Wire fees may apply.
  3. Mobile Deposit via the Earnest App, Convenient, though fees may apply.

Let Kelsie know which method works best and she'll coordinate with the title company to get you instructions.

⏰ Deadline: 4 calendar days from contract acceptance. Do not miss this.
⚠️ Wire Fraud Warning, Please Read
  • Confirm wire instructions by calling the title company directly using their publicly listed number
  • Never trust wire instructions sent only by email
  • Never trust a caller providing new or updated wire instructions

When in doubt, call Kelsie first.

Stage 10

Review These Documents Carefully

Seller Property Condition Disclosure (SPCD)

The seller is required to disclose any known issues with the property. Sign and initial to confirm you received and read it. Important: signing does not mean you agree with the condition of the home, it only documents that you received it.

HOA Documents & CC&Rs (if applicable)

Read carefully. These outline monthly fees, rules, and restrictions. No signature required, just read and flag anything that raises a question.

Preliminary Title Report

The title company will send this showing the plat map, ownership on record, and any recorded liens. Open it, read it, and reply to confirm receipt. Forward anything confusing to Kelsie.

Your To-Do List

  • Sign and initial the SPCD
  • Read HOA documents if applicable
  • Reply to title company to confirm receipt of title report

Stage 11

The Appraisal, What to Expect

Your lender will coordinate with you to order the appraisal. An appraiser will visit the home and determine its market value based on comparable sales in the area. For the full picture on how appraisal and financing fit together, read Kelsie's financing and appraisal guide.

You don't need to be there. The seller or listing agent will coordinate access.

Two Possible Outcomes

  • Appraisal comes in at or above purchase price, you're good to go, nothing changes.
  • Appraisal comes in low, this means the home appraised for less than your offer price. Kelsie will walk you through your options, which may include negotiating with the seller, making up the difference, or in some cases canceling the contract depending on your REPC terms.

Appraisals typically take 1, 2 weeks to complete. Your lender will notify you when it's in.

Stage 12

Clear to Close, The Magic Words

Clear to Close means your lender has fully reviewed and approved your loan. This is one of the best things you'll hear during this process.

What It Means

  • Your income, assets, credit, and the property have all been verified
  • The underwriter has signed off
  • You are approved to close

What Happens Next

  • Title will prepare your final closing disclosure
  • You'll review the closing disclosure, compare it to your loan estimate and flag anything that looks different
  • Closing will be scheduled, usually within a few days

At this point you are on the finish line. Do not make any large purchases, open new credit accounts, or change jobs before you close. Even at this stage it can affect your loan.

Setting Up Utilities Before You Move In

Schedule phone-based utilities (electric, gas, water) to turn on the day of signing. For services requiring installation (internet, cable, security) wait until after possession. Kelsie's full moving and utilities checklist lists every provider and account to update.

Stage 13

Final Walkthrough, This Is a Confirmation, Not an Inspection

No earlier than 7 days before closing, you have the right to walk through the property to confirm it's in the same condition as when you went under contract.

What You're Checking

  • All agreed-upon repairs have been completed
  • Nothing has been removed that was supposed to stay (appliances, fixtures, etc.)
  • Property is broom clean
  • No new damage has occurred

Bring your inspection report and walk through every room. Run the appliances, turn on faucets, flush toilets, check the garage door. If something is wrong, contact Kelsie immediately, do not wait until closing day.

Important: From contract acceptance through closing, the seller cannot make major alterations to the property, take on new financial encumbrances, or accept new short-term rental bookings without your written consent.

Stage 14

Closing Day, You Made It

For a deeper walkthrough of what happens at the signing table, read Kelsie's settlement day guide.

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID
  • Wire confirmation (if you haven't already wired funds)

What to Expect

  • Plan for 1, 2 hours of signing
  • Title will walk you through every document
  • Once everything is signed and recorded, keys are yours

Possession and Keys

Possession follows the terms in your REPC. If your contract states "possession upon recording," the seller releases keys only after the deed has been recorded with the county.

If signing happens later in the day, recording may not happen until the next business day. For example, signing Friday afternoon may mean recording, and keys, on Monday. The county processes recordings in order received and this cannot be rushed. Kelsie will keep you updated the moment it records.
